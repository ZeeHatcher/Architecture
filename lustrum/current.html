Current Projects

Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology

A campus designed to be fit for a world class educational and research institution. With a plan prioritizing strong mutual interconnectedness between people, society, and nature, the residential zone is positioned in close proximity to the housing of existing local residents with the idea of building relationships with the surrounding community. Buildings were positioned according to the contours atop the mountain ridges, leaving valleys intact in order to also prevent damage to natural ecosystems.

Location Okinawa, Japan
Site Area 366,491.94 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 56,605.83 sq.m.
Building Height 27.14 m

POLA Museum of Arts

Nestled in a forest, this art museum exhibits and houses a huge number of works of art, focusing on impressionist paintings. Owing to its location deep within the forest of a national park, consideration was given to achieving coexistence with nature without harming the beauty of the surrounding environment. The majority of the building is underground, and the central lobby of the museum is a bright space, with natural light streaming in from the skylight in the atrium four floors above.

Location Kanagawa, Japan
Site Area 56,572.24 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 8,098.04 sq.m.
Building Height 8.00 ｍ

HOKI Museum

Japan’s first museum dedicated to realist painting. Its design melds with its surroundings, creating exhibition spaces suited to the viewing of highly detailed paintings in a museum that itself could even be considered part of the owner’s collection. It is constructed of gently curved tubes stacked one on top of the other, with 30 meter cantilevers that capture attention.

Location Chiba, Japan
Site Area 3,862.72 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 3,722.39 sq.m.
Building Height 7.06m
