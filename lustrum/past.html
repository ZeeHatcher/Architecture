Past Projects

Tokyo Midtown

This mixed-use urban development totals roughly 560,000 m2 in size, offering a combination of office, hotel, residential, and shops in addition to museums and multi-purpose halls. We have created facilities that can host all types of events, and where local residents can come to relax in an environment with various large and small open spaces within and around buildings that surround the landmark high-rise Midtown Tower. Nikken Sekkei served as the core architect for this project, coordinating the efforts of several design architects.

Location Tokyo, Japan
Site Area 68,891.63 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 563,801.02 sq.m.
Building Height 248.10 m
Completion 2007

Haneda Chronogate

This massive logistics terminal aims to create new value and provide new services in logistics. Along with cutting-edge features in logistics and the offices that support them, there are also community facilities such as an adjacent, wide-open forum, a child daycare center, and café. With a curtain wall that actually visualizes distribution, cutting-edge environmentally-friendly technology, and more, Haneda Chronogate revolutionizes the very concept of logistics.

Location Tokyo, Japan
Site Area 98,872.87 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 197,575.57 sq.m.
Building Height 48.60 m
Completion 2013

YAMAHA Ginza

The flagship store of a worldwide manufacturer of all types of musical instruments. Our design sought to express the brand of the company with the theme of a “building that exudes the feeling of music.” With a diagonal cable grid that tilizes newly-developed gold leaf laminated glass that evokes the image of brass wind instruments, the exterior expresses musical rhythm and variation with subtly changing light reflections.

Location Tokyo, Japan
Site Area 865.57 sq.m.
Total Floor Area 9,079.55 sq.m.
Building Height 66.00 m
Completion 2010
